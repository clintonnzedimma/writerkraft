Africa my Africa

David Diop

Africa my Africa

David Diop on Sep 29, 2017 at 9:48 am

Africa, my Africa
Africa of whom my grandmother sings
On the banks of the distant river
I have never known you
But your blood flows in my veins
Your beautiful black blood that irrigates the fields
The blood of your sweat
The sweat of your work
The work of your slavery
Africa, tell me Africa
Is this you, this back that is bent
This back that breaks
Under the weight of humiliation
This back trembling with red scars
And saying yes to the whip under the midday sun
But a grave voice answers me
Impetuous child that tree, young and strong
That tree over there
Splendidly alone amidst white and faded flowers
That is your Africa springing up anew
Springing up patiently, obstinately
Whose fruit bit by bit acquires
The bitter taste of liberty.
Editor's Note

Born in Bordeaux, Fench West African Poet David Diop wrote poetry that reflected hope of an Africa free of colonial rulers, and in support of African Independence.

In his Poem 'Africa my Africa', we can depict his pain, his struggle for understanding, and his yeraning for the freedom of black slaves.
